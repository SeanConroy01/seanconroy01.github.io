Project 01 - UpComing

Countdown to the moments that matter

Project overview.

It's the experiences in life that matter most. UpComing helps you remember and count down to all those special moments. Whether that be an anniversary, birthday, concert, holiday or wedding, see how many years, months, weeks, days, hours, minutes, and seconds they are away.

The concept behind UpComing was to develop an iOS application that would allow individuals to track how long they had until their various upcoming events. The application needed to have a better user interface than the many existing apps already on the App Store. It could also include additional features to change the events background colour, add custom images, a dark mode, and the ability to change the units of time displayed.

The application was developed in Swift using the model view controller (MVC) design pattern and took approximately two months to develop. The application has been temporarily removed for the App Store to give me time to add some additional features and fix some identified issues.

Further information.

This project allowed me to gain further experience using Swift and Xcode to develop iOS apps. Specifically, gain experience implementing push notifications, interacting with Realm databases and setting up in-app advertisements with Google AdMob. I also gained experience and knowledge of submitting Apps to the App Store and how the review process works to get an application approved.

Reviewing how the project went, there were elements that well, elements that could have been improved and elements that I would do completely differently next time. For example, the development of the function used to calculate how long until a specific date went well. Initially, I thought this would be the most tedious and time-consuming aspect of the project; in reality, it was one of the more straightforward elements. The localisation for the app to accommodate multiple languages could have gone better as it dramatically impacted the process used and the amount of time required to integrate new features into the application. Finally, I would completely change how I advertised/promoted the app as the strategy I used was ineffective, resulting in fewer downloads.
